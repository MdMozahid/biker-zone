Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Age: Manufacturers typically recommend replacing helmets every 3 to 5 years, even if they appear to be in good condition. Over time, the materials in helmets can degrade, reducing their ability to protect your head

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Inspection: Before riding your bike, thoroughly inspect it for any signs of damage or wear. Check the frame, wheels, tires, brakes, and other components for rust, cracks, loose bolts, or other issues that may have developed during storage.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

1.Wear a helmet: Always wear a properly fitting helmet to protect your head in case of a fall or accident.
2.Obey traffic laws: Bicyclists are considered vehicles on the road and must follow the same traffic laws as motorists. This includes obeying traffic signals, stop signs, and road markings.
